Bloom — Privacy Policy

Datenschutzerklärung · Information under Articles 13 and 14 GDPR

Last updated: 19 July 2026 · Impressum / Legal notice

The short version

  • Your health and cycle data stays on your iPhone. We don't upload it and we can't read it.
  • There is no account — no email, no password, no name on our servers.
  • We don't sell your data, don't run ads, and don't track you across apps or websites.
  • When you chat with the coach, a small, anonymised summary of your training is sent for processing so it can answer — including to the USA. Details below.
  • Delete the app and your data goes with it.

2. Health data and your explicit consent

Menstrual cycle information, symptoms and fitness metrics are data concerning health under Article 9(1) GDPR and receive special protection.

We process them only on the basis of your explicit consent under Article 9(2)(a) GDPR, which you give by granting the relevant iOS permission and by entering the data. You can withdraw consent at any time with effect for the future — revoke the permission in iOS Settings, or delete your data via Account → Remove account. Withdrawal does not affect the lawfulness of processing carried out beforehand.

3. What stays on your device

The following is stored only in Bloom's private storage on your iPhone and is never transmitted to us: your name as you type it; your cycle information and predicted phase; daily check-ins (energy, cramps, sleep, flow); your training plan and completed sessions; your run history including routes, distance, pace and heart rate; any trusted contact you pick for the safety feature; and your notification preferences.

Because this data never reaches us, we cannot access, export or delete it for you — you control it directly. Deleting Bloom removes all of it.

5. The AI coach — exactly what leaves your phone

When you message the coach, or Bloom builds your week, we transmit:

  • What you typed in the chat.
  • A derived summary: current cycle phase, recent symptom scores, this week's planned sessions, fitness level, race goal.

We deliberately do not transmit your name, calendar or period dates, location, routes, run history, Apple Health records, or any device or advertising identifier.

Transfer to a third country (Art. 44 ff. GDPR)

Generating the coach's reply involves transferring your message and training summary to Anthropic in the USA. The USA is not covered by a general adequacy decision. The transfer is safeguarded by Standard Contractual Clauses under Article 46(2)(c) GDPR, as incorporated into our data processing agreement with Anthropic.

You should be aware that US authorities may in principle have access rights that do not correspond to the EU standard, and that enforcing your rights there may be more difficult. If you would rather not have data transferred, do not use the AI coach — every other feature of Bloom works entirely on your device.

6. Automated decision-making and profiling

Bloom adapts your training plan automatically from your cycle phase and symptoms, and the AI coach generates suggestions. This is automated processing, and adapting a plan to your data constitutes profiling in the sense of Article 4(4) GDPR.

It does not produce legal effects or similarly significantly affect you within the meaning of Article 22(1) GDPR: the output is a training suggestion you are free to ignore, change or override at any time. Bloom is not a medical device and gives no medical advice. If you describe symptoms suggesting a medical problem, the coach is instructed to tell you to contact a clinician rather than coach you through it.

7. Storage on your device (§ 25 TDDDG)

Bloom stores information on your device solely to deliver the functions you have expressly requested. This is strictly necessary within the meaning of § 25(2) no. 2 TDDDG, so no separate consent banner is required. We do not use cookies, tracking pixels, SDK analytics or advertising identifiers.

8. Your rights

  • Access (Art. 15), rectification (Art. 16), erasure (Art. 17), restriction (Art. 18) and data portability (Art. 20).
  • Objection (Art. 21) to processing based on legitimate interest — here, the request counter.
  • Withdraw consent (Art. 7(3)) at any time, with effect for the future.
  • Complain to a supervisory authority (Art. 77) — in Germany, the data protection authority of your federal state, or ours: the Berliner Beauftragte für Datenschutz und Informationsfreiheit, Alt-Moabit 59–61, 10555 Berlin.

Practically: nearly all your data is on your device, so access and erasure are immediate via the app. For the anonymous request counter, email us — because the identifier is anonymous and not linked to you, we cannot find your record from your email address alone and will ask you to supply the identifier from the app. Where we genuinely cannot identify you, we may be unable to act on a request (Art. 11(2) GDPR).

9. Children

Bloom is not directed at children. We do not knowingly process data from anyone under 16.

10. Security

Data on your device is protected by iOS. All transmission uses encrypted HTTPS/TLS. By keeping health data on your phone we have deliberately minimised what a breach of our servers could expose — there is very little there to take.
